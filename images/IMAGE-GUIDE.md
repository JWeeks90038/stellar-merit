# IMAGE PLACEMENT GUIDE

This guide shows you where to place your statue images for the website.

## Required Images

### 1. Hero Carousel Images (images/hero/)
These appear in the rotating carousel on the home page:
- **meditating-dog-1.jpg** - Feature image of a meditating dog statue
- **owl-1.jpg** - Feature image of an owl statue
- **collection.jpg** - Wide shot showing multiple statues or your studio

**Recommended size:** 1920x1080px (landscape, 16:9 ratio)
**Format:** JPG or PNG

---

### 2. Product Images (images/products/)
These appear in the shop gallery and on the home page featured section:

**Meditating Dogs:**
- **meditating-dog-1.jpg** - First dog statue
- **meditating-dog-2.jpg** - Second dog statue
- **meditating-dog-3.jpg** - Third dog statue

**Owls:**
- **owl-1.jpg** - First owl statue
- **owl-2.jpg** - Second owl statue
- **owl-3.jpg** - Third owl statue

**Optional Other Statues:**
- **buddha-1.jpg** - Buddha statue (if available)
- **garden-gnome-1.jpg** - Garden gnome statue (if available)

**Recommended size:** 800x800px (square, 1:1 ratio)
**Format:** JPG or PNG
**Note:** Product images work best with a clean, neutral background

---

### 3. About Page Images (images/about/)
These appear on the About page:
- **artist.jpg** - Photo of you (the artist) or your logo
- **studio.jpg** - Your workspace/studio where you create
- **process-1.jpg** - Work in progress or sculpting process
- **process-2.jpg** - Finished pieces or another process shot

**Recommended size:** 800x600px (4:3 ratio)
**Format:** JPG or PNG

## Image Tips

1. **Lighting**: Use natural light or soft lighting to avoid harsh shadows
2. **Background**: Clean, neutral backgrounds work best (white, gray, or outdoor settings)
3. **Angles**: Show multiple angles of each statue if possible
4. **Quality**: Use high-resolution images and compress them for web (aim for under 200KB per image)
5. **Consistency**: Try to maintain similar lighting and background across product images

---

## Using Placeholder Images

If you don't have images ready, you can use placeholder images temporarily:
- Visit https://placeholder.com or https://picsum.photos
- Download placeholder images in the sizes mentioned above
- Replace them with your actual photos when ready

Example placeholder URLs you could use:
- Hero: `https://via.placeholder.com/1920x1080`
- Products: `https://via.placeholder.com/800x800`
- About: `https://via.placeholder.com/800x600`

---

## Image Optimization

Before adding images to the website:
1. Resize images to the recommended dimensions
2. Compress images using tools like:
   - TinyPNG (https://tinypng.com)
   - ImageOptim (for Mac)
   - Squoosh (https://squoosh.app)
3. Save product images as JPG with 80-90% quality
4. Aim for file sizes under 200KB for fast loading

---

## Need Help?

If you need help with image editing or optimization, consider using:
- **Free Tools**: GIMP, Paint.NET, Photopea (online)
- **Paid Tools**: Adobe Photoshop, Affinity Photo
- **Mobile Apps**: Snapseed, Adobe Lightroom Mobile
